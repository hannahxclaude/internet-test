WHO SOLD OUT ARIZONA?

The Corporate Interests Behind SB 1350

In 2016, the Arizona Legislature passed SB 1350, stripping cities and counties of the power to regulate short-term rentals in their own communities. It didn't happen by accident.

The bill was model legislation written by ALEC — the American Legislative Exchange Council — a corporate-funded organization that connects lobbyists directly with state legislators to craft pro-business laws. The same bill was pushed in statehouses across the country.

The Players

ALEC (American Legislative Exchange Council)

The Ghostwriter

ALEC is a nonprofit that brings together corporations and state legislators — almost exclusively Republicans — to draft "model legislation" that benefits corporate interests. SB 1350 was based on ALEC's template, and nearly identical bills were introduced in multiple states the same year.

After SB 1350 passed, ALEC's Tax and Fiscal Policy Task Force adopted it as official model policy for other states to follow.

Sen. Debbie Lesko

The Sponsor — ALEC "Legislator of the Year" 2016

Then-State Senator Debbie Lesko (now a U.S. Congresswoman) sponsored SB 1350. For her work passing the bill, ALEC named her their 2016 Legislator of the Year.

ALEC praised Lesko as "the most competent, no-nonsense State Chair who does the best job of bringing members to meetings, representing ALEC to her fellow legislators."

The bill passed the Senate with only one vote against it.

Airbnb & Expedia

The Beneficiaries

Airbnb and Expedia (which owns Vrbo) lobbied heavily for the bill and have continued lobbying against any reforms. In 2020, Arizona mayors sent a letter directly to the CEOs of both companies asking them to "immediately end lobbying activities designed to prevent reform of this disastrous state law which your industry promoted."

Gov. Doug Ducey

The Signature

Governor Ducey signed SB 1350 into law, making Arizona one of only six states to preempt local control over short-term rentals. The others: Florida, Idaho, Indiana, Tennessee, and Wisconsin.

The Vote

SB 1350 passed with overwhelming bipartisan support. Every Democratic lawmaker who voted on the bill voted in favor. Only one senator voted against it.

This wasn't a partisan failure — it was a lobbying success. Corporate interests outspent and out-organized Arizona communities, and legislators from both parties went along with it.

The question now: Will your representatives stand with Arizona families, or continue protecting out-of-state corporate profits?
